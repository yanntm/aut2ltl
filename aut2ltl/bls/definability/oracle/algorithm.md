# Deciding LTL-definability ‚ÄĒ the syntactic ŌČ-semigroup oracle

The oracle decides, for the ŌČ-regular language `L` of a `Language`, whether an
LTL formula defining `L` exists. It emits no formula; it answers the decision
problem exactly, with a checkable certificate on the negative side:

| answer | grounds |
|---|---|
| **LTL** | the syntactic ŌČ-semigroup of `L` is aperiodic ‚ÄĒ a theorem |
| **NOT_LTL** | a **counting family** was extracted and replayed against the input ‚ÄĒ a finite, representation-independent, machine-checkable certificate |
| **INCONCLUSIVE** | a resource cap was hit (determinization, monoid size, time) ‚ÄĒ never an algorithmic blind spot |

Both decided answers are theorems; abstention is only ever exhaustion.

## The characterization

For ŌČ-regular languages the classical equivalences hold (Thomas 1979; Perrin
1984; Perrin‚ÄďPin, *Infinite Words*; Diekert‚ÄďGastin, *First-order definable
languages*, 2008):

```
LTL-definable  =  FO[<]-definable  =  star-free  =  syntactic ŌČ-semigroup aperiodic
```

**Aperiodic = group-free**: no element `s` has a non-trivial cyclic orbit
`s, s¬≤, ‚Ä¶, s^{a+p} = s^a` with period `p > 1`. A non-trivial group is exactly
the ability to count modulo `p`, which counter-free LTL cannot express.

The syntactic ŌČ-semigroup `S(L)` (Arnold 1985) is the canonical invariant of
the language ‚ÄĒ independent of any automaton chosen to present it. The oracle
computes its semigroup part `S(L)‚āä` as a quotient of a finite monoid read off
a deterministic recognizer, and reads aperiodicity off the quotient. Because
the quotient *is* the invariant, the reading is exact in both directions.

## The input

One deterministic form of the language: `det_generic_minimal()` ‚ÄĒ determinized
to deterministic generic-acceptance (Emerson‚ÄďLei), completed. Write `D` for
this automaton, `Q` its states, `C` its acceptance marks, `ő£ = 2^AP` its
alphabet. Completion adds at most a sink ‚ÄĒ an idempotent, invisible to every
algebraic reading below. No minimality is assumed anywhere.

## The certificate: counting families

A negative answer is not left as an algebraic claim; it is handed out as the
object that any third party can check by membership queries alone. Two shapes:

```
linear    F‚āĀ(u, v, x, p) :  n ‚Ü¶ [ u¬∑v‚ĀŅ¬∑x ‚ąą L ]         toggles with n mod p
ŌČ-power   F‚āā(u, v, y, p) :  n ‚Ü¶ [ u¬∑(v‚ĀŅ¬∑y)^ŌČ ‚ąą L ]     toggles with n mod p
```

with `p > 1`; `u`, `v`, `y` finite words; `x` an ultimately-periodic ŌČ-word (a
lasso). "Toggles with `n mod p`" means the membership function is determined
by `n mod p` for **all** `n ‚Č• 0` and is not constant. Every sample of either
shape is an ultimately-periodic word, so both shapes are checkable by
membership queries alone.

**Soundness (either shape ‚üĻ `L` is not LTL).** If `L` were star-free, `S(L)`
would be aperiodic, so `[v‚ĀŅ]` would be eventually constant in `n`, making both
membership functions eventually constant ‚ÄĒ contradicting a genuine period
`p > 1` holding for all `n`. The argument is independent of any automaton and
of the machinery that produced the family.

**Completeness of the pair (two shapes, and no third).** The syntactic
congruence of an ŌČ-regular language separates two finite words by exactly two
context shapes ‚ÄĒ linear `x¬∑_¬∑y¬∑t^ŌČ` and ŌČ-power `x¬∑(_¬∑y)^ŌČ` (Arnold 1985). If
`L` is not star-free, some `[v]` has a cycle of period `p > 1` in `S(L)`; two
of its powers are separated by a context of one of the two shapes, and
unrolling that separation along the cycle yields a toggling family of the
corresponding shape. Hence `L` is not LTL **iff** a family of shape F‚āĀ or F‚āā
exists; no further shape is ever required.

**Both shapes are load-bearing.** If `L` is prefix-independent (`ŌÉw ‚ąą L ‚üļ
w ‚ąą L` for every finite `ŌÉ`) then `u¬∑v‚ĀŅ¬∑x ‚ąą L ‚üļ x ‚ąą L`: every linear family is
constant, on every choice of `(u, v, x)`. Prefix-independent non-LTL languages
exist ‚ÄĒ e.g. *"infinitely many `¬¨a` and eventually every `a`-block has even
length"* (`samples/fixtures/hoa/definability/evenblocks_nonltl.hoa`), whose
count is exhibited by the ŌČ-power family `(a‚ĀŅ¬∑¬¨a)^ŌČ` and by no linear one.

## The recognizer: the acceptance-enriched monoid

The algebraic handle on `D` must remember what acceptance reads: the marks
visited *along* a run, not only its endpoints. The **acceptance-enriched
monoid** `EM(D)` does exactly that. The element of a finite word `w` is the
map

```
q  ‚Ü¶  ( őī(q, w),  marks collected from q along w )
```

with composition `(f, M)¬∑(g, N) : q ‚Ü¶ (g(f(q)), M(q) ‚ą™ N(f(q)))` ‚ÄĒ a
transformation monoid on the finite set `Q √ó 2^C`, generated by the letters.
It is materialized by BFS from the letter generators, keeping one shortest
concrete word as the representative of each element.

**The skeleton lemma.** Two finite words with the same enriched element are
interchangeable in every ŌČ-context: any run through either lands in the same
states *and* collects the same marks per start state, so ‚ÄĒ `D` being
deterministic ‚ÄĒ the run skeleton of every surrounding ŌČ-word is identical, and
so is its acceptance. Consequently the syntactic morphism `ő£‚Āļ ‚Üí S(L)‚āä`
factors through `ő£‚Āļ ‚Üí EM(D)`, and the induced map

```
EM(D)  ‚ĒÄ‚Ėļ  S(L)‚āä
```

is a surjective morphism: `EM(D)` recognizes `L`, and `S(L)‚āä` is a computable
quotient of it. (The transition monoid ‚ÄĒ the same object with the marks
forgotten ‚ÄĒ does *not* recognize `L` in this sense: two words with equal state
maps but different intermediate marks can be separated by an ŌČ-power context.
The enrichment is what makes the quotient below correct.)

## The congruence

By the skeleton lemma, Arnold's two context shapes act on words only through
their enriched elements, so the syntactic congruence reduces to a relation on
the finite monoid ‚ÄĒ for `e, f ‚ąą EM(D)`:

```
e ~ f   iff   ‚ąÄ a, b ‚ąą EM¬Ļ, t ‚ąą EM :   Acc(a¬∑e¬∑b, t) = Acc(a¬∑f¬∑b, t)      (linear)
        and   ‚ąÄ a, b ‚ąą EM¬Ļ         :   Acc(a, e¬∑b)  = Acc(a, f¬∑b)         (ŌČ-power)
```

where `Acc(x, c)` is the acceptance of any ŌČ-word `w¬∑z^ŌČ` with `[w] = x`,
`[z] = c`, from the initial state ‚ÄĒ well defined by the lemma, and computable
with no query on the automaton: land via `x`'s state map, iterate `c`'s state
map until the orbit closes, union `c`'s collected marks around the closed
cycle, evaluate the Emerson‚ÄďLei condition on that inf-set. `EM¬Ļ` adjoins the
identity (the empty context); `t` ranges over non-identity elements (an ŌČ-word
needs a non-empty cycle).

**Theorem.** `EM(D)/~  =  S(L)‚āä`. Hence `L` is LTL-definable iff `EM(D)/~` is
aperiodic ‚ÄĒ and a group in the quotient is never an artefact of the
presentation, because the quotient is presentation-independent.

## The procedure

1. **Screen (sound shortcut).** If the transition monoid `TM(D)` is aperiodic,
   `L` is star-free (Thomas 1979: a counter-free deterministic automaton whose
   acceptance is a function of the inf-set recognizes a star-free language) ‚ÄĒ
   answer **LTL** without building `EM`. The converse fails ‚ÄĒ a group in
   `TM(D)` can be an artefact of the deterministic encoding (e.g. `GF(a ‚ąß Xa)`
   has a 2-state deterministic recognizer whose letter `a` is a transposition:
   `samples/fixtures/hoa/definability/gf_aa_parity.hoa`) ‚ÄĒ so a group here
   decides nothing and the procedure continues.
2. **Materialize `EM(D)`** by BFS under a size cap; blowing the cap is
   **INCONCLUSIVE**.
3. **Signatures.** For each element `x`, the trivial-context acceptance data
   `ŌÉ(x) = ( t ‚Ü¶ Acc(x, t) ;  a ‚Ü¶ Acc(a, x) )` ‚ÄĒ `x` as the finite prefix
   against every cycle, and `x` as the cycle behind every prefix. The initial
   partition groups elements by `ŌÉ`.
4. **Refinement.** The congruence is the coarsest two-sided congruence
   refining the `ŌÉ`-partition: split a class whenever left- or
   right-translation by a letter generator maps two of its members into
   different classes; iterate to fixpoint (at most `|EM|` splits). **Each
   split records its cause** ‚ÄĒ the side, the generator, and the finer split
   (or base `ŌÉ`-difference) it appealed to ‚ÄĒ so that chaining the records
   turns any two non-congruent elements into a *concrete* separating context
   of one of the two shapes: the distinguishing-word construction of DFA
   minimization, transported to a semigroup.
5. **Aperiodicity.** Power-iterate each class of the quotient until its orbit
   closes. Every period 1 ‚üĻ answer **LTL**.
6. **Extraction (a group ‚üĻ a family, guaranteed).** Pick a class `[v]` with
   period `p > 1`; its power cycle consists of `p` pairwise *distinct* ‚ÄĒ
   hence pairwise *separated* ‚ÄĒ classes. Retrieve a separating context for
   one pair from the split records; instantiate through the BFS
   representatives: a linear context `(a, b, t)` gives `F‚āĀ` with `u = rep(a)`,
   `v = rep([v])`, `x = rep(b)¬∑rep(t)^ŌČ`; an ŌČ-power context `(a, b)` gives
   `F‚āā` with `y = rep(b)`. Membership of the `n`-th sample depends only on
   the class of `v‚ĀŅ`, so the pattern is eventually periodic; absorbing the
   index into the anchor (`u ‚Üź u¬∑v^index`) makes it exactly periodic from
   `n = 0`. The **declared period** is the minimal cyclic period of the
   membership pattern read around one cycle (`p` cheap `Acc` evaluations) ‚ÄĒ
   non-constant because the chosen context separates. Nothing here can come
   home empty: the certificate is a by-product of the quotient computation,
   not a search.
7. **Replay.** The family is material, not yet a verdict: it is replayed by
   membership against the *input* automaton (the engine-agnostic verifier,
   in-process, bounded ‚ÄĒ sampling `n = 0 ‚Ä¶ 2p` and checking the pattern is
   `p`-periodic and non-constant). Replay guards the transport between the
   discovery form and the input (letter rendering, composition order); the
   toggle itself was established structurally in step 6. Success ‚üĻ answer
   **NOT_LTL** with the family attached. A replay failure signals transport
   corruption: the family is discarded and the answer degrades to
   **INCONCLUSIVE** ‚ÄĒ a decided answer is never built on unreplayed material.

## Cost

The dominant object is `|EM(D)|`, bounded by `(|Q|¬∑2^{|C|})^{|Q|}` ‚ÄĒ the
`|Q|` in the exponent is where the explosion lives; the size cap converts it
into an honest **INCONCLUSIVE**. On top of materialization, the `ŌÉ`-pass is
`O(|EM|¬≤)` `Acc` evaluations, each an `O(|Q|)` state-map walk (memoizable per
cycle element), and refinement costs `O(|EM|¬∑|ő£|)` per split. Determinization
up front is the unchanged entry toll of every consumer of a deterministic
form.

An opening on the explosion: enriched elements are relations on `Q √ó 2^C`,
and both the BFS closure and the refinement are set-of-elements fixpoints ‚ÄĒ
the shape symbolic reachability (BDD-represented relations, fixpoints over
sets) is built for. A symbolic `EM` would attack the `|Q|` exponent directly;
nothing in the congruence or the extraction depends on elements being
enumerated explicitly, only on (a) closure, (b) `Acc` per element pair,
(c) split bookkeeping.

## Modules (design)

- `congruence.py` ‚ÄĒ the `ŌÉ`-signatures, the two-sided refinement, the split
  records and separating-context retrieval.
- `oracle.py` ‚ÄĒ the entry: pull and complete the form, run the screen,
  materialize `EM` under the cap, quotient, power-iterate; on a group,
  assemble the family from the split chain, replay, and return the
  three-outcome verdict.

The enriched monoid (elements, composition, shortest-representative BFS) is a
shared primitive of the `definability` package.

## Layering

Above the floor (`Language`), the letter extractor, and the engine-agnostic
verifier; imports neither `Cascade` nor any `Translator`. Consumer: the
definability gate, which owns verdict plumbing (absorbing vs. non-absorbing)
around whatever the oracle answers.
