# The language-adapter algorithm

A Translator wrapping the kanchor read-off. From the outside it is nothing
more than that — a language in, a result out, freely composable in
recipes; its internals are its own business.

## Setting

A translator maps a language to a label:

```
Label       =  Some φ  |  NotLTL(w)  |  ⊥    -- φ an LTL formula; w a non-LTL witness; ⊥ = decline
Translator  =  Language → Label
```

`⊥` is recoverable (a chooser tries the next member); `NotLTL(w)` is an
absorbing verdict about the language itself, carrying its counting-family
witness.

kanchor's preconditions are **presentation predicates**: they are tested
on one automaton, `sbacc(tgba(L))`, while the property that matters is
existential — *some* state-based presentation of `L` has a k-anchored
initial component (`../algorithm.md`, layer 12: form dependence).
Sampling a single presentation loses coverage to form luck: `GF(a ∧ Xa)`
anchors at k = 2 as its minimal DBA and is hopeless as its natural
2-state NBA — the same language, one stay/move ambiguity apart.

Contrary to most translators, this one therefore does not inspect one
form of its input and answer or decline: it explores **several
presentations of the language, in order**, and delegates each to kanchor
through a dedicated hook,

```
core  :  twa [, context]  →  Label           -- the read-off after form acquisition
```

bypassing kanchor's usual Translator input (a `Language`). The bypass is
the point: handing a prepared automaton back as a `Language` would send
it through form acquisition again, re-canonicalizing on the way in and
possibly undoing exactly the form that was built. The hook feeds it
untouched. kanchor's public entry keeps its meaning unchanged — it *is*
the first presentation below.

Two rules hold for every presentation:

- **Language-preserving by construction.** Each is a transformation that
  provably preserves the ω-language (a Spot postprocess, a
  determinization, a one-letter peel). Nothing that would need an
  equivalence gate is admitted; the adapter inherits kanchor's no-gate
  exactness end to end.
- **Bounded or skipped.** Each production runs under a budget; one that
  stalls or explodes is skipped and recorded in the diagnosis, never
  waited on.

## The presentations, in order

1. **Identity** — `sbacc(tgba(L))`, the form the bare brick reads. First
   and free: whatever anchored without the adapter anchors through it, at
   the same cost.
2. **Deterministic** — the state-based form under Spot's deterministic
   preference, taken only when the result is actually deterministic and
   generalized Büchi within budget. This is the biggest lever:
   in-component nondeterminism from one source — a letter that both loops
   and moves out of the same state — is invisible to a window of *any*
   width (the two readings share their entire context), so no k rescues
   it in place, while the language may well have a deterministic
   presentation whose phase is transparent. The phase lemma needs at most
   one run per word inside `C`; this presentation is where that run is
   procured.
3. **The start peel**, on the deterministic form — when the component
   fails only at its entry (the 0-step start test, or an initial state
   whose out-guards tangle), one letter of the word is peeled into the
   label and the remainder is re-presented with that letter as known
   history. Detailed below.

The exploration is the choice monoid of `result.py` applied to
presentations instead of translators: the first non-declined answer
returns — `OK`, or `NotLTL` at *any* presentation, since a counting
family witnesses a property of the language, not of a form — and a
decline falls through to the next; exhaustion declines, naming the
presentations tried. The list is short by design: each entry earns its
place by a failure class it provably converts, not by being one more
canonical form to try.

## The start peel

Position 0 is the one place a window construction is structurally blind:
an entry at position `p < k` has only `p` letters of history, and the
start machinery (P0², the truncated rows) is the price. The peel removes
the blindness instead of pricing it:

```
Final  =  ⋁_cells ( g_cell ∧ X φ_cell )
```

where the cells partition the letters at position 0 by the **overlap
algebra of `q0`'s out-guards** — the atoms generated by the guards, a set
bounded by the out-degree, never by `2^AP` — and `φ_cell` labels the
cell's successor language, explored by the same presentations. On the
deterministic form each cell has a single successor state, so the child
is a plain reroot; this is why the peel runs on presentation 2, not 1. A
`NotLTL` child lifts across the peeled letter by the standard peeler move
(`result.prefix`), exactly as kanchor lifts across its exits.

- **The context handoff.** The peeled letter is not forgotten: the child
  delegation passes it as known history — `core(twa, context)` — and the
  truncated window classes are built from it. A context letter is
  stronger than a full window's `I(v)`-weakened component (an actual
  letter, not a stutter abstraction), so with `k−1` letters of context
  every entry is fully windowed: the start law, the truncated parks and
  leaves, and the P0² test dissolve — the label is the pure steady-state
  transcription. This mirrors, inward, what the witness lift
  (`../algorithm.md`, layer 11) does outward: the lift prepends a
  reaching word to carry a verdict out; the peel prepends a known word to
  carry certainty in. Both are letter-level and window-width-blind.
- **The depth bound.** At most `k−1` consecutive peels — a k-window can
  consume no more history than that, so further peeling adds cost and
  nothing else. The bound is also what makes the peel terminate: peeling
  is not a reduction (`a^ω` peels to `a ∧ X a^ω`, the same language), so
  without the saturation bound the recursion would regress forever. At
  k ≤ 2 the peel fires at most once.
- **Staging.** The peel without the handoff (the child explored blind) is
  already sound — the child's fresh canonical form may pass where `q0`
  tangled — but it re-faces the start problem one state deeper; the
  handoff is what closes it. The `context` parameter of the hook is
  therefore part of this algorithm, staged after the presentations:
  1 and 2 need no change to the brick at all.

## Reporting

The presentation that answered is part of the answer: it stamps a
technique tag of its own (identity adds nothing, the others tag the form,
e.g. `…@det`, `…@peel`), so a survey row attributes the answer to the
form that produced it without any log archaeology. A decline lists the
presentations tried and each skipped one's reason — an exhausted
exploration is "not anchored in the forms tried", never "not anchorable".

## Out of scope

- **Presentation caching on `Language`.** The adapter rebuilds its forms
  per call; whether `Language` should memoize presentations is a later,
  separate decision — the algorithm deliberately asks nothing new of it.
- **Reverse-canonical forms.** Phase recoverability is a *past* property
  and every presentation above is future-canonical (determinization and
  minimization merge states by future behavior — occasionally the merge
  that destroys transparency). Whether a co-deterministic presentation is
  systematically kinder to anchoring is a real question, parked until an
  example forces it.
- **Measurement.** Which presentations fire on which corpora, and what
  they cost, is survey business, read off the technique tags.
