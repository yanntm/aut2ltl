## Part 1 вҖ” В§3.2, full text

### 3.2 Semantics: the language of an invariant

An invariant decides lassos with the data it carries and nothing else: `О»`
assigns each letter its class, the algebra's `M` extends that assignment to
every finite word вҖ” stem and loop alike вҖ” and `P` lists the pairs that accept.
But В§3.1 is syntax only: for an invariant to act as a recognizer of a
language, its acceptance layer must obey a law. This section extends `О»` to
words (the fold), states the law (saturation), and proves the law is exactly
what a semantics requires: a saturated invariant gives every lasso one
verdict вҖ” it recognizes a language (Theorem 3.6).

**Definition 3.3 (fold).** Let `рқ’ң = (рқ’һ, О», M)` be an algebra over `ОЈ`. The
**fold** of `рқ’ң` is the map `вҹҰВ·вҹ§ : ОЈ* вҶ’ рқ’һ` extending the letter map to all finite
words through the algebra: for `u = xвӮҒxвӮӮвӢҜxвӮҷ вҲҲ ОЈ*`,
`вҹҰuвҹ§ := О»(xвӮҒ)В·О»(xвӮӮ)В·вӢҜВ·О»(xвӮҷ)`, the empty product being `вҹҰОөвҹ§ := О»(Оө) = [Оө]`; we
call `вҹҰuвҹ§` the fold of `u`.

The fold is well defined: `M` is a total function and associative
(Definition 3.1), so the product of the letter classes always exists and its
value does not depend on how it is parenthesized вҖ” one class per word. It is
moreover a monoid morphism вҖ” `вҹҰuВ·vвҹ§ = вҹҰuвҹ§В·вҹҰvвҹ§`, `вҹҰОөвҹ§ = [Оө]` вҖ” the only one
agreeing with `О»` on the letters: on nonempty words it is В§2's morphism `ПҶ`,
realized on the algebra, and the adjoined `[Оө]` extends it to the empty word.

*Example.* On Figure 1 (`aUGb`), the fold of a word is where its reading ends вҖ”
one letter, one edge, from the root: `вҹҰaabвҹ§ = [a]В·[a]В·[b] = [aВ·b]`, and
`вҹҰbaвҹ§ = [b]В·[a] = [bВ·a]`, the dead class.

**Word classes.** Call a class a **word class** when it is the fold of some
nonempty word. The word classes are closed under `M` вҖ” a product of folds is
the fold of the concatenation вҖ” and computable by one breadth-first walk of the
letter actions from `О»(ОЈ)`. In the canonical invariants of В§3.3 the word
classes are exactly `рқ’һ вҲ– {[Оө]}`; an arbitrary algebra may also carry classes
that no word folds to вҖ” inert data, as the semantics below makes plain вҖ” and
may fold nonempty words onto its identity.

**The law.** A lasso does not choose its presentation:
`uВ·v^Пү = (uv)В·v^Пү = uВ·(vВІ)^Пү = (uВ·vвӮҒ)В·(vвӮӮВ·vвӮҒ)^Пү` whenever `v = vвӮҒВ·vвӮӮ` вҖ” one
Пү-word, cut differently. Whatever acceptance `P` encodes must not depend on the
cut, and the moves that change a cut act on classes through one schema вҖ” a
factor carried from the loop's front onto the stem,
`uВ·vвӮҒВ·(vвӮӮvвӮҒ)^Пү = uВ·(vвӮҒvвӮӮ)^Пү`, a **rotation**. The law closes `P` under it.

**Definition 3.4 (conjugacy; saturation).** Over an algebra `рқ’ң`, call two
pairs of classes **conjugate** when they are connected by the equivalence
generated by the **rotation steps**

```
    (s, (cВ·d)^Пү)  вҲј  (sВ·c, (dВ·c)^Пү),        s, c, d word classes with sВ·(cd)^Пү = s.
```

A pair set `P` is **saturated** when it is closed under conjugacy вҖ”
equivalently, under each rotation step:

```
    (s, (cd)^Пү) вҲҲ P   вҹә   (sВ·c, (dc)^Пү) вҲҲ P.
```

A **saturated invariant** is an invariant whose pair set is saturated.

Both sides of a rotation step are linked pairs вҖ” `(cd)^Пү` is idempotent by
construction and `s` absorbs it by hypothesis; that the rotated pair is linked
too is an algebra identity proved with Lemma 3.7. Stem extension is the
degenerate rotation `c = d`: the loop's value is unchanged and the stem absorbs
one turn. Conjugacy of linked pairs is classical [PP04, Ch. II, Prop. 2.6];
saturation is checkable on the algebra вҖ” finitely many triples `(s, c, d)`,
each one product and two lookups.

*Example.* On Figure 1 (`aUGb`), every conjugacy class is a singleton вҖ”
whatever factor a rotation moves, the dead class absorbs it, and the two
accepting stems absorb their loops вҖ” so every pair set over this algebra is
saturated, Figure 1's included: each of the 2вҒ¶ subsets of its six linked pairs
is a legal acceptance layer. The law has teeth on other algebras: the
discussion below builds the minimal offender, and `Even` (В§3.4) houses a
genuine two-name conjugacy inside a useful invariant.

**Definition 3.5 (presentation; query).** Let `рқ“ҳ = вҹЁрқ’ң, Pвҹ©` be an invariant
over `ОЈ`. A **presentation** of a lasso `Оұ вҲҲ ОЈ^Пү` is a pair `(u, v)` with
`u вҲҲ ОЈ*`, `v вҲҲ ОЈвҒә` and `Оұ = uВ·v^Пү`. The presentation **folds to** the pair of
classes `(вҹҰuвҹ§В·e, e)`, where `e := вҹҰvвҹ§^Пү` is the idempotent power of the fold of
the loop; and the **query** of `(u, v)` on `рқ“ҳ` **accepts** iff that pair is
listed:

```
    (u, v)  accepted   iff   (вҹҰuвҹ§В·e, e) вҲҲ P,      e := вҹҰvвҹ§^Пү.
```

Say a pair of classes **names** the lasso `Оұ` when some presentation of `Оұ`
folds to it. A name is a linked pair вҖ” `eВІ = e` by construction, and
`(вҹҰuвҹ§В·e)В·e = вҹҰuвҹ§В·e` вҖ” and a pair of word classes: pick `k вүҘ 1` with
`вҹҰvвҹ§^k = e`; then `e = вҹҰv^kвҹ§` and `вҹҰuвҹ§В·e = вҹҰuВ·v^kвҹ§`, folds of nonempty words.
Pairs of `P` that name no lasso вҖ” non-linked pairs, pairs touching a class no
word folds to вҖ” are inert: no query ever consults them.

*Example.* On Figure 1 (`aUGb`), the two presentations we have been reading
since В§2. For `(aab, b)`: the loop folds to `вҹҰbвҹ§ = [b]`, already idempotent, so
`e = [b]`; the stem folds to `вҹҰaabвҹ§ = [aВ·b]` and `[aВ·b]В·[b] = [aВ·b]`. The pair
`([aВ·b], [b])` is in `P`: the query accepts. For `(ba, ab)`: the loop folds to
`вҹҰabвҹ§ = [aВ·b]`, not idempotent вҖ” its square `[bВ·a]` is вҖ” so `e = [bВ·a]`; the
stem folds to `[bВ·a]` and `[bВ·a]В·[bВ·a] = [bВ·a]`. The pair `([bВ·a], [bВ·a])` is
not in `P`: rejected, as В§2 announced.

**Theorem 3.6 (recognition).** Let `рқ“ҳ = вҹЁрқ’ң, Pвҹ©` be an invariant. The query
gives every presentation of every lasso one verdict iff `P` is saturated. The
accepted lassos of a saturated `рқ“ҳ` are then exactly the lassos of the regular
language

```
    L(рқ“ҳ)  :=  вӢғ { foldвҒ»В№(s)В·(foldвҒ»В№(e) вҲ© ОЈвҒә)^Пү  :  (s, e) вҲҲ P,  eВ·e = e,  sВ·e = s },
```

the unique regular language with those lassos: `рқ“ҳ` **recognizes** `L(рқ“ҳ)`.

Both directions of the iff, and the language identity, rest on the names of
lassos, and the rest of the section earns them: a witness that saturation is
necessary (Lemma 3.7), a chase showing it suffices (Lemma 3.8), then the
assembled proof.

**Why the law is exactly right.** First, the violation, made minimal. Let `рқ’ө`
be the **mod-2 counter** over `ОЈ = {a, b}`: three classes
`рқ’һ = {[Оө], [a], [aВ·a]}`, both letters sharing a class вҖ” `О»(a) = О»(b) = [a]` вҖ”
and products `[a]В·[a] = [aВ·a]`, `[aВ·a]В·[a] = [a]`, `[aВ·a]В·[aВ·a] = [aВ·a]`: the
fold of a word is the parity of its length. The only idempotent word class is
`[aВ·a] = [a]^Пү`, and the linked pairs of word classes are `([a], [aВ·a])` and
`([aВ·a], [aВ·a])`. Now present `a^Пү` twice: `(Оө, a)` folds to
`([Оө]В·[aВ·a], [aВ·a]) = ([aВ·a], [aВ·a])`, while `(a, a)` folds to
`([a]В·[aВ·a], [aВ·a]) = ([a], [aВ·a])` вҖ” one Пү-word, two names, one per parity of
the stem. A pair set holding one name and not the other вҖ” say
`P = {([aВ·a], [aВ·a])}` вҖ” answers the single word `a^Пү` both yes and no: no
verdict, no language. The flaw is visible in what the fold counts: the parity
of the *cut* between stem and loop вҖ” and the cut is the presentation's choice,
not the word's. The law forbids exactly this: the rotation step at
`(s, c, d) = ([aВ·a], [a], [a])` connects the two names, so a saturated `P`
holds both or neither. The saturated pair sets over `рқ’ө` are exactly `вҲ…` and
the full two-pair set, recognizing `вҲ…` and `ОЈ^Пү` вҖ” the two languages blind to
the cut. That `ОЈ^Пү` here rides on three classes where two suffice (В§3.3) is a
different excess вҖ” presentation, not contradiction вҖ” and canonization, not the
law, removes it.

`рқ’ө` violates by hand; the general fact is that *every* rotation step is
witnessed by a lasso, so no unsaturated `P` ever survives:

**Lemma 3.7 (rotation).** Let `s, c, d` be word classes of an algebra `рқ’ң` with
`sВ·(cd)^Пү = s`. Then `(sВ·c, (dc)^Пү)` is a linked pair, and some lasso is named
by both `(s, (cd)^Пү)` and `(sВ·c, (dc)^Пү)`.

*Proof.* First the algebra identities. Associativity gives
`cВ·(dc)^m = (cd)^mВ·c` for every `m вүҘ 1`. Pick `kвӮҒ, kвӮӮ вүҘ 1` with
`(cd)^{kвӮҒ} = (cd)^Пү` and `(dc)^{kвӮӮ} = (dc)^Пү`, and set `m := kвӮҒВ·kвӮӮ`: then
`(cd)^m = ((cd)^{kвӮҒ})^{kвӮӮ} = (cd)^Пү` and likewise `(dc)^m = (dc)^Пү`, so
`cВ·(dc)^Пү = (cd)^ПүВ·c`. Hence `(dc)^Пү` is idempotent and
`(sВ·c)В·(dc)^Пү = sВ·(cd)^ПүВ·c = sВ·c`: the rotated pair is linked. Now pick
nonempty words `u, vвӮҒ, vвӮӮ` with `вҹҰuвҹ§ = s`, `вҹҰvвӮҒвҹ§ = c`, `вҹҰvвӮӮвҹ§ = d` вҖ” they
exist, the three classes being word classes вҖ” and consider the single Пү-word
`Оұ := uВ·(vвӮҒvвӮӮ)^Пү`. The presentation `(u, (vвӮҒvвӮӮ)^m)` folds to
`(sВ·(cd)^Пү, (cd)^Пү) = (s, (cd)^Пү)`; the presentation `(uВ·vвӮҒ, (vвӮӮvвӮҒ)^m)` вҖ” the
same Пү-word, `uВ·(vвӮҒvвӮӮ)^Пү = uВ·vвӮҒВ·(vвӮӮvвӮҒ)^Пү` вҖ” folds to
`(sВ·cВ·(dc)^Пү, (dc)^Пү) = (sВ·c, (dc)^Пү)`. So `Оұ` is named by both. вҲҺ

The missing half is that *all* names of one lasso are conjugate, and it is a
chase of three moves:

**Lemma 3.8 (the names of a lasso).** In any algebra, any two names of one
lasso are conjugate.

*Proof.* Three preliminary observations. *(Powering.)* For `k вүҘ 1`, the
presentations `(u, v)` and `(u, v^k)` fold to one pair: the powers of
`вҹҰv^kвҹ§ = вҹҰvвҹ§^k` are powers of `вҹҰvвҹ§`, each set holds exactly one idempotent, so
`вҹҰv^kвҹ§^Пү = вҹҰvвҹ§^Пү =: e`; and the stems agree, `вҹҰuвҹ§В·e` both. *(Absorption.)*
`eВ·вҹҰvвҹ§ = вҹҰvвҹ§В·e` вҖ” powers of one element commute. *(One turn.)* For any word
class `s` with `sВ·e = s`, the rotation step at `(s, вҹҰvвҹ§, вҹҰvвҹ§)` вҖ” legal: `вҹҰvвҹ§`
is a word class and `(вҹҰvвҹ§В·вҹҰvвҹ§)^Пү = e`, so the stem absorbs вҖ” connects `(s, e)`
to `(sВ·вҹҰvвҹ§, e)`.

Now let `(u, v)` and `(u', v')` present one lasso `Оұ`, say `|u| вүӨ |u'|`
(conjugacy is symmetric). Both stems are prefixes of `Оұ` and the tail after `u`
is `v^Пү`, so `u' = uВ·z` with `z` a prefix of `v^Пү`: write `z = v^qВ·vвӮҒ` with
`0 вүӨ |vвӮҒ| < |v|`, and split `v = vвӮҒВ·vвӮӮ` (`vвӮӮ` nonempty). The tail of `Оұ` after
`u'` is then `(vвӮӮВ·vвӮҒ)^Пү`, so `(u', vвӮӮvвӮҒ)` is a third presentation of `Оұ`.

*The names of `(u, v)` and `(u', vвӮӮvвӮҒ)` are conjugate.* Choose `m вүҘ 1` with
`вҹҰvвҹ§^m = e` and `(вҹҰvвӮӮвҹ§вҹҰvвӮҒвҹ§)^m = (вҹҰvвӮӮвҹ§вҹҰvвӮҒвҹ§)^Пү =: f` simultaneously (the product
trick of Lemma 3.7). Applying *(one turn)* `q` times connects `(вҹҰuвҹ§В·e, e)`, the
name `(u, v)` folds to, with `(вҹҰuвҹ§В·вҹҰvвҹ§^qВ·e, e)`, the name `(uВ·v^q, v)` folds
to. If `vвӮҒ = Оө` that presentation is `(u', v)` and `vвӮӮvвӮҒ = v`: done. Otherwise
one rotation step at the triple `(вҹҰuвҹ§вҹҰvвҹ§^qВ·e, вҹҰvвӮҒвҹ§, вҹҰvвӮӮвҹ§)` вҖ” legal: `вҹҰvвӮҒвҹ§` and
`вҹҰvвӮӮвҹ§` are word classes, and the stem absorbs `(вҹҰvвӮҒвҹ§В·вҹҰvвӮӮвҹ§)^Пү = вҹҰvвҹ§^Пү = e` вҖ”
connects it further to `(вҹҰuвҹ§вҹҰvвҹ§^qВ·eВ·вҹҰvвӮҒвҹ§, f)`, and that pair is the name
`(u', vвӮӮvвӮҒ)` folds to: by *(absorption)* and `cВ·(dc)^m = (cd)^mВ·c`,

```
    вҹҰuвҹ§вҹҰvвҹ§^qВ·eВ·вҹҰvвӮҒвҹ§  =  вҹҰuвҹ§вҹҰvвҹ§^{q+m}В·вҹҰvвӮҒвҹ§  =  вҹҰuвҹ§вҹҰvвҹ§^qВ·вҹҰvвӮҒвҹ§В·(вҹҰvвӮӮвҹ§вҹҰvвӮҒвҹ§)^m  =  вҹҰu'вҹ§В·f.
```

*The names of `(u', vвӮӮvвӮҒ)` and `(u', v')` are equal.* Both loops repeat to the
same tail, `(vвӮӮvвӮҒ)^Пү = v'^Пү`; the prefix of that tail of length `|v'|В·|vвӮӮvвӮҒ|`
is a common power, `v'^{|vвӮӮvвӮҒ|} = (vвӮӮvвӮҒ)^{|v'|}` as finite words, and
*(powering)* twice gives one name for all four presentations `(u', v')`,
`(u', v'^{|vвӮӮvвӮҒ|})`, `(u', (vвӮӮvвӮҒ)^{|v'|})` and `(u', vвӮӮvвӮҒ)`. Chaining: the
names of `(u, v)` and `(u', v')` are conjugate. вҲҺ

*Proof of Theorem 3.6.* *(Saturated вҹ№ one verdict.)* Two presentations of one
lasso fold to conjugate names (Lemma 3.8); a saturated `P` is closed under the
rotation steps that generate conjugacy, so conjugate pairs are both in or both
out of `P` вҖ” one verdict.

*(One verdict вҹ№ saturated.)* Let word classes `s, c, d` satisfy
`sВ·(cd)^Пү = s`. Lemma 3.7 exhibits one lasso and two of its presentations
folding to `(s, (cd)^Пү)` and `(sВ·c, (dc)^Пү)`; one verdict on that lasso puts
the two pairs on one side of `P`.

*(The accepted lassos are the lassos of `L(рқ“ҳ)`.)* Let `Оұ` be accepted: every
presentation `(u, v)` folds to one pair `(s, e) вҲҲ P`, `e := вҹҰvвҹ§^Пү`. Pick `k`
with `вҹҰvвҹ§^k = e`: the stem block `uВ·v^k` folds to `вҹҰuвҹ§В·вҹҰvвҹ§^k = вҹҰuвҹ§В·e = s`, the
loop blocks `v^k` fold to `e`, so `Оұ = (uВ·v^k)В·(v^k)^Пү` lies in the member of
the union indexed by `(s, e)`. Conversely let `Оұ` be a lasso of `L(рқ“ҳ)`:
`Оұ = wвӮҖВ·wвӮҒВ·wвӮӮвӢҜ` with `вҹҰwвӮҖвҹ§ = s`, `вҹҰw_jвҹ§ = e` for `j вүҘ 1`, `(s, e) вҲҲ P`,
`eВІ = e`, `sВ·e = s`; and let `(u, v)` present `Оұ`. The block boundaries
`b_j := |wвӮҖвӢҜw_j|` are infinitely many, so two of them, `m := b_j < m' := b_{j'}`,
lie beyond `|u|` and agree modulo `|v|`. The prefix `Оұ[0, m) = wвӮҖвӢҜw_j` folds to
`sВ·e^j = s`. Position `m` sits in the `v`-periodic tail at phase
`r := (m вҲ’ |u|) mod |v|`: split `v = vвӮҒВ·vвӮӮ` with `|vвӮҒ| = r`; the tail of `Оұ`
after `m` is `(vвӮӮvвӮҒ)^Пү`, and `m' вҲ’ m` is a multiple of `|v|`, so the segment
`Оұ[m, m') = (vвӮӮvвӮҒ)^{(m'вҲ’m)/|v|}` вҖ” nonempty, and folding to `e^{j'вҲ’j} = e`.
Thus `(Оұ[0, m), Оұ[m, m'))` is a presentation of `Оұ` whose loop folds to the
idempotent `e` directly: it folds to `(sВ·e, e) = (s, e) вҲҲ P`. One of `Оұ`'s
presentations accepts, hence вҖ” one verdict вҖ” all do.

*(Regularity and uniqueness.)* Each `foldвҒ»В№(c)` is recognized by the finite
monoid `(рқ’һ, M)` through the fold morphism (В§2), hence regular; `L(рқ“ҳ)` is a
finite union of sets `XВ·Y^Пү` with `X, Y` regular вҖ” Пү-rational, hence regular
[PP04, Ch. II, Thm 7.5]. Two regular Пү-languages with the same lassos are equal
[PP04, Ch. I, Cor. 9.8]. вҲҺ

Theorem 3.6 delivers the section's title: for a saturated invariant вҖ” and for
it alone вҖ” `L(рқ“ҳ)` is a language, the union its denotation, the query its
decision procedure: fold the stem, fold the loop to its idempotent power, one
lookup in `P`. Classes that no word folds to never enter the verdict вҖ” an
invariant may carry them, inertly; it is canonicity, not semantics, that they
disturb (two saturated invariants recognizing one language must not differ by
inert data if equality is to be read off the components), and В§3.3's
canonization is where they are discarded. What is still missing is one
recognizer per language; that is В§3.3's business.
