# SoS Learner — Paper Data Specification

**Rev 2026-07-19.** This spec defines the machine-generated data products behind
every figure and table of the paper (`sos_learning.md`), and nothing else. The
algorithm's normative statement is the paper §3–§5; testing, gates, and the
harness are engineering-internal to `sosl/`. The development-era spec lives in
git history.

## Corpus

`genaut/corpus/flat_canon` — 6222 ω-regular languages, one per language up to
AP relabeling, closed under complement, each with its precomputed reference
invariant. Reach cases via `manifest.flat_canon_cases()`; resolve duals via
`manifest.dual_index` — never construct a `<id>_c` name (the catalogue grows,
and growth renames). Every census statistic must be dual-symmetric at sweep
completion. Named cases (the paper's worked examples): `even`, `evenblocks`,
`gf_aa_parity`, `gf_aa_reset`, plus the proven-permanent specimens
`a_implies_xa` and `a_once` (paper Prop. 4.4).

Budget convention: the `SOUND` / `ACCEPTOR_ONLY` / `BUDGET` split depends on
what a machine finishes inside the per-case budget, so the paper reports the
partition **at its stated budget** — decided counts are floors, `BUDGET` rows
are undecided, and no decided case ever flips. Nothing more is needed.

## Provenance (normative)

Every number in the paper traces to a committed, machine-generated file under
`reference/`, cited next to the table that uses it and regenerated by the
command stated here (run from `sosl/`). `logs/` is scratch and never citable.
Hand-edited data does not exist. The corpus grows, so the committed drop — not
a re-run — is the reproduction; census drops are additive (`--done`).
